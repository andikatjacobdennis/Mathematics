<h3>Roman Letters</h3>
<p>The roman letters are the English alphabets but not all the alphabets are the roman alphabets. There are 23 roman letters out of 26 English alphabets, in which J, U and W are not considered as roman. Hence, the roman letters are:<br />
A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, K, L, M, N, O, P, Q, R, S, T, V, X, Y and Z.<br />
All these letters are used to represent the roman numbers. For example, the year 2019 is written as MMXIX.</p>
<p><b>Converting Roman Numerals to Numbers</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">We can easily convert any numbers into a roman form and conversely, any roman form into any number.</span> <span style="font-weight: 400;">Let us understand with the help of an example, Consider a number, 1984</span></p>
<p><b>Convert number into the roman form</b></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Break the number 1984 into 1000, 900, 80 and 4, then perform each conversion</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">As, 1000 + 900 + 80 + 4 = 1984</span></p>
<ul>
<li>1000 = M</li>
<li>900 = CM</li>
<li>80 = LXXX</li>
<li>4 = IV</li>
</ul>
<p>1000 + 900 + 80 + 4 = 1984,</p>
<p>So, <b>1984 = MCMLXXXIV</b><br />
<a id="rules-of-forming" name="Rules of Forming"></a></p>
<h3>Rules for forming the Numbers</h3>
<ul>
<li>When a symbol appears after a greater symbol, it will be added. For Example-  VI = V + I = 5 + 1 = 6</li>
</ul>
<ul>
<li>When a symbol appears before a greater symbol, it will be subtracted. For Example-  IX = X – I = 10 – 1 = 9</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Tips &#8211; </strong><strong>Break the number into Thousands, Hundreds, Tens and Ones, and write down each in turn</strong></p>
<p><strong>Convert 1774 to Roman Numerals</strong></p>
<p>Break 1774 into 1000, 700, 70, 4 and then do each conversion</p>
<p>1000 = M</p>
<p>700 = DCC</p>
<p>70 = LXX</p>
<p>4 = IV</p>
<p>1000+700+70+4 = 1774, so 1774 = <strong>MDCCLXXIV</strong><br />
<a id="conversion-rules" name="Conversion Rules"></a></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Rules for Converting Numbers to Roman Numerals</span></h2>
<p>The three primary rules for writing and reading Roman numerals:</p>
<p><strong>1.</strong> <span style="font-weight: 400;">A letter can be repeated only for three times, for example XXX = 30, CC = 200, etc.</span></p>
<p><strong>2.</strong> If one or more letters are placed after another letter of greater value, add that amount.</p>
<p>VII = 7 (5 + 2 = 7)</p>
<p>LXXX = 80 (50 + 10 + 10 + 10 = 80)</p>
<p>MCCC = 1300 (1000+100 + 100 + 100 = 1300)</p>
<p><strong>3.</strong> If a letter is placed before another letter of greater value, subtract that amount.</p>
<p>CM = 900 (1000 – 100 = 900)</p>
<p>IX = 9 ( 10 &#8211; 1 = 9 )</p>
<p>XC = 90 (100 – 10 = 90)</p>

<div class="faq-section mb30" id="Frequently-Asked-Questions-–-FAQs"><h2><span style="color: #800080;">Frequently Asked Questions – FAQs</span></h2><div class="faq-schema"><div itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/FAQPage"><section itemscope="" itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question"><h3 itemprop="name text">How do we write 100 in roman?</h3><div itemprop="suggestedAnswer acceptedAnswer" itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><div itemprop="text">In roman numerals, 100 is represented by C.</div></div></section><section itemscope="" itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question"><h3 itemprop="name text">Write roman numerals from 1 to 10.</h3><div itemprop="suggestedAnswer acceptedAnswer" itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><div itemprop="text">The roman numerals from 1 to 10 are given by:<br/>
1 – I<br/>
2 – II<br/>
3 – III<br/>
4 – IV<br/>
5 – V<br/>
6 – VI<br/>
7 – VII<br/>
8 – VIII<br/>
9 – IX<br/>
10 – X</div></div></section><section itemscope="" itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question"><h3 itemprop="name text">How do we write 200 in Roman?</h3><div itemprop="suggestedAnswer acceptedAnswer" itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><div itemprop="text">200 in Roman is written as CC.</div></div></section><section itemscope="" itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question"><h3 itemprop="name text">How do you write roman numbers?</h3><div itemprop="suggestedAnswer acceptedAnswer" itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><div itemprop="text">Roman numbers are written by using seven different alphabets. The numbers and alphabets are:<br/>
I – 1<br/>
V – 5<br/>
X – 10<br/>
L – 50<br/>
C – 100<br/>
D – 500<br/>
M – 1000<br/>
Using these alphabets, we can write all the roman numbers.</div></div></section><section itemscope="" itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question"><h3 itemprop="name text">What does the roman number XC equal to?</h3><div itemprop="suggestedAnswer acceptedAnswer" itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><div itemprop="text">XC = 90</div></div></section><section itemscope="" itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question"><h3 itemprop="name text">How to evaluate 800 in roman numeral?</h3><div itemprop="suggestedAnswer acceptedAnswer" itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><div itemprop="text">800 = 500 + 100 + 100 + 100<br/>
500 = D and 100 = C<br/>
Therefore,<br/>
800 = DCCC</div></div></section><section itemscope="" itemprop="mainEntity" itemtype="https://schema.org/Question"><h3 itemprop="name text">What is the use of roman numerals?</h3><div itemprop="suggestedAnswer acceptedAnswer" itemscope="" itemtype="https://schema.org/Answer"><div itemprop="text">Roman numerals are used for labelling the name or position of any object or a person.<br/>
For example, Sam came Ist in class.<br/>
Prince Charles III<br/>
